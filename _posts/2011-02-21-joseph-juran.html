Joseph Juran made the assertion in 1941 that 80% of the effects come from 20% of the causes and dubbed the principle "Pareto's Principle" (after the early 20th century Italian economist Vilfredo Pareto who observed this law in action in farmland distribution). 

Pareto's law was only meant to apply to large systems (big business, economies, society, etc), so it's really not meant to be a lens for individuals, but I think it works just fine. It's one of my favorite "rules of thumb." It is exceedingly simple, but I've found that it works in almost every facet of life:

  • 80% of work comes from 20% of clients
  • 80% of headache comes from 20% of things in your life
  • 80% of revenue comes from 20% of sources
  • 80% of success comes from 20% of your time

The list goes on and on. 

The more I thought about it though, Pareto's law is certainly valuable as a designer, but also just as a human :) The four examples I gave above are applicable to a designer, but also to most working people. I find in general, I like to apply the 80/20 rule when I start to get overwhelmed.

I start by identifying what 20% is causing 80% of my stress, or vice versa, what 20% is causing 80% of my joy. 

Once I've worked out those factors, I work hard to minimize my stress and maximize my enjoyment. It sounds overly simple, but it really works well for me. Just give it a try next time you're wigging out with too much to do and I'm sure you will be surprised how effective it is.
