Twitter can be a loud, crazy place if you follow more than 100 people. I recently made a conscious effort to follow more designers from varying backgrounds, interests + focuses, but my problem was my Twitter feed was becoming overwhelming.

I don't handle noise well and often resolve to just tuning everything out, which means I lose a lot of quality content in the clutter.  That's not to say a lot of the people I am following are pushing tweets I don't want to read, but rather that the differences in content make them hard to read side by side. Suppose I just woke up and want to know what crackin' in the design community, I'm forced to also read through tweets from all the other people/businesses I follow. Thus, the brilliance of Twitter lists.

By organizing the people/companies I follow into digestible lists that aggregate types of content, I'm more comfortable increasing my follow count.

I have been reticent to follow more Twitter peeps since my feed was already becoming somewhat noisy, but allowing me to essentially create filters frees me of concern. I have a number of private lists, but to name a few for example:

  • Designers: Members of the design community I think are crushing it (Dave Desandro, Matthew Smith, Trent Walton...so many more)
  • Design Resources: Accounts that push a lot of tutorial, best of lists and opinion content (Design Follow or SmashingMag)
  • Friends + Family (Hear what's going on in my personal circle)

Twitter lists are a necessary feature (for those who follow quite a few peeps), but the next step is going to be a better set of interactions for managing those lists and making them easy to use.
